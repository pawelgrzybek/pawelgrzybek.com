I have been a loyal customer of 1Password since 2013. It served me well and I never really looked into the alternatives. I didn't mind occasionally paying for an upgrade to the newer version, or even switching to subscription model a few years ago.

!!! Screenshot of my apple store from 2013

In the recent years though, my list of frustrations with 1Password was growing. It all started when 1Password migrated from the native build to the electron version of their desktop app. Things became sluggish. Not slow, but not natively fast. The browser extension became unreliable as well (random locks and syncing delays) but I had no choice other than keep on using it. The load time of the app became almost as bad as Photoshop when I used it last time.

Generating strong passwords and keeping them safely is all I expect from a password manager. I got it 13 years ago, I have it now, and can't care less about long list of new features added to new versions. The most recent "upgrade" to the app caught my attention though and inspire me to switch to Apple Passwords.

!!! Screenshot of the recent email from 1Password

## Why Apple Passwords

The list of alternatives is huge, but [I like defaults](/my-defaults-2023/). At this point I'm so locked to the Apple ecosystem so locking myself to it even more is not a concearn anymore, just a risk I accepted long ago. Apple Passwords is free, and it's gonna be hard to beat this price.

## What's good and bad

I have been using Apple Passwords for over a week now, and I have some thoughts, things I like and dislike. I also like lists so here you go, two lists!

### Good:

- auto fill is so fast
- app is so fast, feels like

### Bad:
